# Glossary

Canonical language for the decisions and artifacts produced by these agent skills.

**Experiential question**:
A question judged by looking or trying — layout, hierarchy, interaction flow, tone — and settled through reactions to a concrete build. Its complement, a propositional question, settles in prose.
_Avoid_: Perceptual decision

**Prototype**:
A disposable build that makes experiential uncertainty directly judgeable before production commitment: variants compared at a known fork, a strawman drafted while preferences are latent, or the confirmed composition materialized for final review.
_Avoid_: Wireframe as the umbrella term

**Production commitment**:
The point at which a confirmed direction is rewritten to delivery quality and retained in the product's main implementation path.
_Avoid_: Implementation, because disposable implementation may be part of the prototype

**Variant**:
One of a small set of alternatives that differs on the governing decision while holding confirmed constraints fixed. Losing variants are discarded once the decision lands.
_Avoid_: Mockup, option

**Issue artifact**:
The single artifact kept per issue that accumulates confirmed experiential decisions. Its final state is the session's deliverable and is not production code.
_Avoid_: Preserved prototype
