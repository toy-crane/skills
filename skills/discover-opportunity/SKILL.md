# Discover Opportunity

Draw out what the user already knows but has not yet recognized as an
opportunity.

Explore concrete experiences: work they have done, situations they repeatedly
notice, people they understand, strengths others rely on, and interests that
persist. Ask for examples and reflect the patterns back as possible ways their
knowledge could be useful. Do not make the user invent an idea from a blank
page.

Let the conversation branch or narrow according to the user's reactions. Find
a direction that feels meaningful and is grounded in something the user knows;
leave product design and validation for later.

When a direction emerges, state what the user knows, who or what situation it
could help, and the change it might enable. Continue directly with
`shape-idea` using that context. Do not create a document unless the user asks
for one.

If no direction resonates, stop honestly.
